    This is How Char-Broil Transformed Itself Into a Data-Driven Company With Revuze

    Learn how the Georgia-based innovative charcoal grills company shifted its culture so everyone uses data to make intelligent decisions, and how Revuzes AI-powered dashboard helped facilitate this much-welcomed change.

    One of the first to bring charcoal grills to market

    Char-Broils incredible story starts in 1948, when they brought over one of the first charcoal grills to the US, changing grilling forever. Since then, the Georgia-based company has kept innovating with new products and technologies. All in the name of improving outdoor cooking for everyone, everywhere.

    THE CHALLENGE

    The challenge of understanding consumers wants and needs

    The Char-Broil team understood that consumer reviews were critical to gaining a deeper understanding of their customers. Once they overcome this, theyll be able to improve their ability to respond to consumer needs, requests, and concerns.

    The key challenge was to get that feedback on products in real-time and then relay it to development teams according to their timelines so theyll develop new products and features to meet dynamic market demand.

    For a company thats looking to build a consumer data-obsessed company culture, this was a real thorn on their side.

    One of the key challenges is real-time feedback and being able to continuously get that feedback loop within an adequate amount of time.

    Rob Hawkings

    Senior Marketing Manager @Char-Broil

    Old-school methods of data-collection and analysis werent cutting it

    At first, Char-Broils attempt to deliver data-driven decisions was mainly powered by a full-time data analyst on staff. They focused on capturing and analyzing voice-of-customer (VoC) data.

    But the company found it challenging, as data was hoarded within a single department and wasnt flowing fast enough to meet their needs.

    Further, individual product managers and engineers conducted independent VoC exercises. Still, the company didnt have a centralized customer review database or an analysis tool to evaluate its competitors or its position in the market.

    We see similar challenges across all industries. Many companies are after data, which is excellent, but often more is needed. You must analyze the feedback in real-time to make an impact. Brands that can't respond quickly enough to their consumers' needs are bound to be left behind.

    Rotem Ben Shitrit

    Head of Customer Success
    @Revuze

    THE SOLUTION

    How Char-Broil used Revuze to control and own their data

    Char-Broil set out to find a resource optimization tool that will solve those challenges and help the company:
    • Formulate a stronger competitive strategy
    • Improve consumer awareness and conversion
    • And transform consumers into brand advocates

    They found it in Revuze

    Within a short time of working with Revuze, the Char-Broil team was able to achieve incredible results that transformed the company.

    Democratized data access and building a collaborative company culture

    Revuzes easy-to-use dashboard allowed Char-Broil to develop a new and open mode of communication across the company. All organizational stakeholders at Char-Broil gained real-time access to VoC data, from product management and engineering to brand/marketing, sales, quality, and consumer service. Team members in all functions can hear the voice of the customer loud and clear, gathering insights objectively to better meet customer needs across the board. Instead of specific roles hoarding data, all team members share what theyve learned from Revuzes dashboard, discussing implications collaboratively. Char-Broil has even developed a cross-functional learning plan in which captains from each discipline work together to create monthly review reports as well as a quarterly Voice of the Consumer review.
